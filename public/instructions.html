How to Play Blackjack

Objective

Get the sum of your cards as close to 21 as possible without exceeding it. You play against the dealer, not other players.

Card Values

Aces are worth 1 or 11 — whichever benefits your hand. Face cards (J, Q, K) are worth 10. All other cards are their pip value.

The Pack

The game uses two standard 52-card decks shuffled together. Cards are dealt from the top of the shuffled deck each round.

The Deal

Each player receives two cards face up. The dealer receives one card face up and one face down. Players then take turns acting on their hand before the dealer reveals their hidden card.

Naturals

If your first two cards are an ace and a ten-card (10, J, Q, or K), that's a natural — a score of 21 in two cards. If the dealer doesn't also have a natural, you win immediately.

Your Actions

Hit
Take another card. You can hit as many times as you like until you stand or bust.
Stand
Keep your current total and end your turn.
Double
Take exactly one more card and end your turn. Only available on your first two cards.
Split
If your first two cards have the same value, split them into two separate hands played individually.

Dealer's Rules

After all players have acted, the dealer reveals their hidden card. The dealer must keep hitting until reaching 17 or more, then must stand. If the dealer busts, all remaining players win.

Winning

You win if your total is higher than the dealer's without busting, or if the dealer busts. You lose if you bust or the dealer's total is higher. Equal totals result in a tie — neither wins.

Basic Strategy Tips

  • Always hit until at least 17 when the dealer shows a 7 or higher.
  • Stand on 12 or more when the dealer shows 4, 5, or 6 — let them bust.
  • Always split Aces and 8s.
  • Never split 10s, 5s, or 4s.
  • With a soft hand (ace + low card), keep hitting until you reach at least 18.
